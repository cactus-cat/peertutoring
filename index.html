Bridging 10 Peer Tutoring Project

Shrivaas Kaushik · Bridging 10 · Ms. Lally

My Introduction to the Course

The course introduced me to the fundamentals of peer tutoring, helped me learn effective communication strategies, deepened my understanding of diverse learning styles, and let me explore best practices in teaching and mentoring.

My Successes

I successfully helped students improve their grades, received positive feedback from mentees, developed stronger teaching confidence, and built lasting relationships with the students I worked with.

My Challenges

My challenges included managing different learning paces and styles, maintaining patience with struggling students, balancing my own studies with tutoring responsibilities, and adapting explanations for more complex concepts.

Multiple Intelligences and Learning Styles

Thinking about multiple intelligences and different learning styles helped me keep the tutoring process grounded and practical. Instead of assuming there was one right way to explain a concept, I stayed aware that people process information differently, so I tried to offer a few different approaches and pay attention to what seemed to make sense. That mindset made it easier to stay flexible, to notice when something was not clicking, and to adjust without overcomplicating the session. It felt more realistic and honest, and it made the whole experience feel less like a scripted method and more like working with the person in front of me.

My Plans

I plan to continue tutoring and mentoring in the future, develop specialized tutoring programs for different subjects, train other peer tutors using my experience, pursue education or psychology in higher studies, and build a community of learners and educators.

My Advice

  • Try to get to know the student so that you can tailor your tutoring to their needs.
  • Be patient and understanding, especially when students are struggling.
  • Use a variety of teaching methods to cater to different learning styles.
